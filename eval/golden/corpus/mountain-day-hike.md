#hiking #outdoors

Prep checklist for a long day hike in the mountains. Carry more water than feels
necessary and electrolytes for the climbs; dehydration ruins the descent. Layer for
weather that changes with altitude — a shell for wind and rain even on a clear
morning. Break in the boots well before the trail and pack blister tape. Bring more
calories than the distance suggests; the elevation gain is the real cost, not the
mileage. Start early to beat afternoon storms, tell someone the route, and carry a
paper map since signal dies between the ridges.
