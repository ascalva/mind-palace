A slow-cooked ragù worth the afternoon. Brown the beef and pork hard for a deep crust,
then soften onion, carrot, and celery in the rendered fat. Deglaze with a glass of red
wine, scrape the fond, add tomato and a splash of milk for body. Then the patience:
the lowest simmer for three hours, barely a bubble, stirring now and then so it
doesn't catch. The sauce should coat the spoon, not run off it. Finish it in the pan
with the pasta and its starchy water so everything clings. Salt in layers, taste often.
