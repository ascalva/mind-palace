Notes on growing tomatoes in the backyard beds. They want at least six hours of
direct sun and deep, infrequent watering rather than a daily sprinkle — wet leaves
invite blight. Mix compost into the soil before transplanting and bury the stem deep
so it roots along its length. Stake or cage them early; once they sprawl it's too
late. Pinch the suckers between the main stem and branches to push energy into fruit.
Mulch holds moisture and keeps the soil from splashing up disease.
