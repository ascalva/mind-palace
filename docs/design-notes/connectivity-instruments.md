# Connectivity instruments: σ*, conductance, bridges, and helices over the temporal substrate

> Composed at **fable** (`claude-fable-5`/xhigh, 2026-07-17, owner-chartered: "draft the design
> note(s) so I can ratify it"). Filed as `draft`; ratification is owner-by-hand; `/graduate`
> refuses this note until ratified. **Design only; no build authorized.** The full derivations,
> proofs, and literature grounding live in the fable pass (C1–C7); this note *decides*. Claims
> here inherit the pass's labels; nothing is asserted beyond what the pass established.

## 1. Purpose and scope

The owner asked four questions of the corpus graph: *given two random thoughts A and B, how do
you connect them? What is the conductivity between two points, when change distorts distance and
the local clock? Can a connection be a coherent argument — a chain of reasoning? Can a cycle
close across time only — a helix?* This note decides the instrument family that answers them:
**σ\* (the abstraction ultrametric), the (σ,t) conductance profile with reconnection events,
type-checked bridges with arc search, and the helix detector** — all read-side, model-free,
derived-tier instruments over the substrate the temporal quartet just completed.

**The substrate inversion (recorded because the owner flagged it, and it is real).** Every
artifact of the just-sealed quartet turns out to be a load-bearing prerequisite of this family,
though none was built for it: certified cuts (bp-055) are the *snapshot legality* every profile
needs; the clock maps (bp-053) supply the *local clocks and pitch* ; the T-meet atlas (bp-056)
is the *cross-clock type-checker for arguments*; the fibers (bp-050) already record the
*σ-filtration data*; the gate (bp-057) established the *two-axis reporting discipline* bridges
inherit; and the spine's acyclicity (bp-051) is the *engine of the helix theorem*. The honest
half of the observation: both were generated by the same algebra, so the convergence is partly
by construction — which is itself the design language doing its job.

**Out of scope:** retrieval/storage *scaling* (compression, compaction, decimation — the
adjacent brainstorm keeps its own future gate, and wants a `data/` growth measurement first);
any dreamer modification; the magnetic/spectral build (its own lane's gate holds); the *design*
of uuid-identity (this note pins its requirement surface and registers a consumer — §2.6 — but
its design is its own act); any build.

## 2. Principles / decisions

### 2.1 CN-1 — the connectivity object and its three times

**Decision.** Every instrument in this family is indexed by a point in **(σ, t, cut)**-space:
σ the abstraction scale (the nested cosine filtration), t the diffusion/walk time inside a fixed
graph, and the cut the corpus-history coordinate. The three axes are never conflated; each instrument **declares its index
signature** over them (σ\*: (σ-grid, cut) — no walk, hence no t; conductance: (σ, t, cut);
bridges: (σ, cut, budget); helix: (cut-window)), and every reading carries its declared tuple. "The graph at a moment" exists **only at a certified cut** (GC-3);
wall-clock indexes nothing (Law C4). The corpus is the two-layer object: undirected σ-indexed
spatial graphs fibered over the acyclic spine — acyclicity is a temporal-layer property only.

*Falsifier:* a reading missing any index its instrument declares, or computed at an
uncertified cut, is malformed — the reading refuses to emit.

### 2.2 CN-2 — σ\*: the abstraction ultrametric and its realizing chain (the keystone)

**Decision.** `σ*(A,B) = sup{σ : A ∼ B in G_σ(cut)}` — the strictest threshold at which two
thoughts still share a component — is the family's first instrument. It is a single-linkage
ultrametric ("how coarse must abstraction get before two thoughts are the same conversation"),
equal to the maximin-cosine path value; **one maximum spanning tree per cut yields all pairwise
σ\* and the realizing chain for every pair** in O(E log V). σ\* is **grid-relative** (the
fibers discipline): computed against the declared σ-grid, the MST built on the loosest-grid
graph, the grid pinned in every reading's evidence (the FibersEvidence pattern); a pair
unconnected there reports **"not connected within grid"** — an honest bounded answer, never an
extrapolation. The MST chain is the canonical
"path of least resistance" — the strongest single chain of association between any two thoughts.

*Falsifiers:* the ultrametric inequality `σ*(A,C) ≥ min(σ*(A,B), σ*(B,C))` on sampled real
triples; MST-derived σ\* equals a direct union-find sweep over the grid (two independent
computations agree).

### 2.3 CN-3 — conductance made honest: the (σ,t) profile and reconnection events

**Decision.** "Conductivity" is effective conductance — computed and reported **only as the
(σ,t) profile**, never as a single dense-graph resistance: raw R_eff provably degenerates to
local degrees in dense regimes (the von Luxburg result), so finite-t diffusion distances are
authoritative there, and **every profile carries the degeneracy self-diagnostic**
(`corr(R_eff, 1/d_A + 1/d_B)` at the loosest σ) — the instrument reports its own domain of
validity. Commute time supplies the operational meaning (expected wander time of a
dreamer-like walk). Rayleigh monotonicity binds: removals can never raise conductance;
therefore **a conductance rise between consecutive cuts requires new edges**, and the
contributing additions are enumerable and individually testable (leave-one-out re-computation)
— the reconnection report names the bridging event(s) it *verified*, never a guess. A **reconnection event** — the owner's
"connections I forgot I had made," made measurable — is a Δ-conductance spike between cuts
across a large proper-time gap, reported *with the bridging event named*.

*Falsifiers:* conductance non-decreasing as σ loosens, per real cut (Rayleigh observed); the
self-diagnostic present on every emitted profile; a synthetic decay-only interval showing no
conductance increase.

### 2.4 CN-4 — the metric slot: churn as a change of measure (owner-refined, 2026-07-17)

**Decision.** "Change distorts distance" enters not as one signed drag but as a **derived
decomposition with signs forced by circuit law** (owner refinement; pass §C4b):

- **The depth budget (the owner's bound, adopted as a law of the family).** Events partition by
  stratum, so `N(W) = Σ_s N_s(W)` — the global budget is additive. Any ≼-chain confined to
  stratum s within window W has ≤ `N_s(W)` events: **proper time is the sequential-depth
  budget** — no thread or argument chain through a region can be deeper than the region's local
  clock ticked over the window it spans (GC-2 supplies the readings today).
- **Sequential churn** (supersession depth; edge turnover) acts in **series** — through-window
  traversal walks the threads against that budget: *impedes, by law*.
- **Lateral churn** (new cross-links) acts in **parallel** — Rayleigh: *conducts, by law*.

Weights: `w(u,v) = cos(u,v)^α · exp(s_lat·a_lat(u,v) − s_seq·a_seq(u,v))` with `α = 1` and
`s_lat, s_seq ≥ 0` all THRESH-dict entries (**α default 1; magnitudes shipped at 0**), and the
v1 edge convention `a_•(u,v)` = mean of the endpoint strata's statistics (revisited at item-2
design review); the components read from the spine — canonical form:
the region's window-event poset depth vs width (Mirsky/Dilworth; sequentiality
`χ_s(W) = longest-chain / N_s(W)`), practical v1: edge-type counts. Only *magnitudes* are
sweepable; the signs are structural. "Metal vs semiconductor" becomes a **measured per-region,
per-window ratio**, not a stance. "Change distorts the local clock" requires no new
construction — GC-2's proper time *is* the local clock, and temporal path-length routes
through it (a churny region as a higher-refractive-index medium: geodesics lengthen through it
unless its churn built lateral shortcuts). Metric curvature (this) and gauge holonomy (§2.6)
remain independent axes (magnetic pass Q5).

*Falsifiers:* the depth budget on the real spine (no ≼-chain within (s, W) exceeds `N_s(W)`
events; `χ_s ∈ (0,1]`, = 1 iff the window-events are totally ordered — synthetic check);
any code path assigning lateral churn an impedance sign or sequential churn a conductance sign
(the signs are law, not tuning); hard-coded nonzero magnitudes outside the THRESH dict;
promotion to `ops/levers.py` without a separate owner-visible act.

### 2.5 CN-5 — bridges: arguments are well-formed paths

**Decision.** A path is an **argument** iff it type-checks: the running meet of its edges'
scopes stays non-empty and admissible — an O(length) check in the ratified algebra — with
**the registered atlas (GC-4) as the cross-clock type-checker**: a chain whose steps live on
atlas-uncovered clocks *refuses* (the anti-hallucination property, now at chain level). Paths
that fail the type-check remain conductance-bearing *associations* — wanted, and graded
separately. A **bridge** `B(A,B)` is reported on **two axes, never fused** (the one-scalar
prohibition, inherited from FB-3): the best well-formed chain (the argument you present) and
the conductance profile (the confidence you have). Bridges are **surfacing/report-layer
only** — never a weight, never a confidence input, never a promotion (I1 re-asserted verbatim).

*Falsifiers:* a chain crossing atlas-uncovered clocks that computes instead of refusing; any
pers/conductance/chain-quality scalar fusion; a bridge writing to any store beyond eval
readings.

### 2.6 CN-6 — the helix: revisitation is necessarily helical

**Decision.** The helix is formalized on the **gain graph** over the idea quotient: spatial
edges carry gain 0 (co-presence); temporal edges carry the **ℤ-valued interval count as the
primary gain** — always defined, strictly positive per forward temporal hop, well-defined even
across strata — with per-stratum proper time (GC-2) as the *refinement* where a hop stays
within one stratum. (The ℤ-primary choice also insulates the detector from the open
finding-0090 proper-time erratum: the refinement waits on its resolution.) The pitch is a
linear functional on the cycle space. **The helix criterion is nonzero pitch, not
snapshot-invisibility**: a citation cycle can be fully co-present at one cut yet carry positive
pitch — a legitimate, separately-reported subclass (a loop closed *by* time while visible *in*
one snapshot). The **forced-helix theorem** (proved in the pass) binds:
*every closed idea-walk traversing ≥1 temporal edge all-forward has strictly positive pitch —
its lift cannot close; revisitation is necessarily helical* — with the spine's constructed
acyclicity as the engine. Purely-spatial cycles are legal and flat; time-balanced mixed cycles
exist; the pitch functional is the proper-time-valued refinement of the magnetic pass's Q3
arm-imbalance flux (reconciliation pinned there; the U(1)/spectral route stays behind the
magnetic lane's own gate, per its Q6). **Detector v1 is combinatorial**: fundamental-cycle
pitches off a spanning tree, O(E); a revisitation helix is reported with pitch (the period) and
winding (the revisit count).

**The prerequisite, pinned.** The projection π needs **uuid-identity** — stable idea-identity
along supersession chains. This note formally registers the **third consumer** (after Track D
and the SF-a claim-identity family; one identity design should serve all three). π's
requirement surface here: total on versioned chunks, constant along supersession chains,
never merging distinct ideas (false merges manufacture phantom cycles — the detector's
integrity depends on π's precision more than its recall).

*Falsifiers:* pitch ≡ 0 on every spatial-only cycle; pitch > 0 on every all-forward
revisitation walk (synthetic first, real once π lands); a **zero-pitch** cycle reported as a
helix (detector bug — snapshot-visibility is explicitly NOT the criterion).

### 2.7 CN-7 — the arc: bidirectional field-guided search with refusal

**Decision.** Query-time bridge search between two *named* endpoints is **bidirectional,
field-guided, budget-bounded** growth (the owner's arc, whose physics — dielectric breakdown —
is guided by the same Laplacian potential as CN-3): frontiers from A and B expand by potential-
guided priority (A\*/reduced-cost form of the same object), **each candidate extension
scope-meet-checked at expansion time** (the streamer only grows through admissible bonds) —
and since composability is a *path* property (the running meet is state), the search is
label-setting over (node, accumulated-scope) states, with dominance pruning sound because
scopes only narrow along a path — until they meet or the budget exhausts. Budget exhaustion yields the principled refusal **"no arc at
this voltage"** — quantified (budget spent, frontier reach), never a partial path presented as
a bridge. v1 is deterministic (the high-η limit = bidirectional Dijkstra); stochastic η-growth
is parked (§4).

*Falsifiers:* the meet-point chain equals the optimal well-formed chain from an exhaustive
reference search on small instances (label-dominance correctness); a below-requirement budget
produces the refusal report, never a partial bridge.

## 3. Consequences — the instrument tranche (what /graduate mints, post-ratification)

Session-sized, in dependency order; estimates in the house unit. All eval-side; write scopes
disjoint from core except where named.

1. **σ\*/MST instrument** (CN-2 + the CN-1 indexing discipline) — new eval/harness module +
   quality tests; consumes the dreamer's σ-graph builder + certified cuts. Falsifiers: §2.2 +
   the Law-C4 tooth. **~150–180k. No new dependencies; the keystone.**
2. **(σ,t) conductance profile + reconnection rider** (CN-3, CN-4) — Laplacian/heat-kernel
   over the same graphs; the `(s_seq, s_lat)` THRESH levers + the `χ_s` sequentiality statistic
   + the depth-budget tooth; Δ-conductance cut-pair scan with event attribution. Falsifiers:
   §2.3 + §2.4. **~200k. Depends on item 1's module surface.**
3. **Bridge search v1** (CN-5 + CN-7) — typed-edge composability + atlas checks + bidirectional
   budget-bounded search; two-axis bridge report. Falsifiers: §2.5 + §2.7. **~200k. Depends on
   items 1–2.**
4. **Helix detector v1** (CN-6) — the idea-quotient gain graph + fundamental-cycle pitches.
   **GATED on uuid-identity landing** (its own design/plan, not this tranche). Falsifiers:
   §2.6. **~180k.**

Family-wide laws binding every item: read-side/derived-tier/model-free (the spine's posture);
no store writes beyond eval readings; certified cuts only; Law C4 (no wall-clock as an ordering
key — AST tooth in every plan); evidence pinning per the FibersEvidence pattern (grid +
fingerprints in every reading); levers THRESH-first.
