\chapter{Philosophy}
\label{ch:philosophy}

This chapter is the \emph{why}. It states the principles the rest of the manual
gives mechanism to --- and it states them as principles, citing the ratified
record and the code, never asserting a mechanism it has not earned. Where a stance
is grounded but its formal design is still being worked out, the chapter names the
stance and forward-references the chapter that will carry the machinery. The
governing habit of the whole book is set here: \emph{assert nothing without a
source} (\artifact{dn-agent-workflow}, \S 3). A claim you cannot check is, in this
system, a defect --- not prose.

\section{The mirror, not the oracle}
\label{sec:mirror}

Start with what the system is \emph{for}, because every later constraint falls out
of it. It exists to serve one person as a \emph{mirror onto their own mind} ---
indexing a private corpus, surfacing patterns across it, helping its owner think
(\coderef{CONSTITUTION.md}{\gitref}, \S I; \coderef{docs/BUILD-SPEC.md}{\gitref},
\S 1). It is a sealed, single-user sandbox. That sentence is not marketing; it is
the north star against which every change is judged, and the test is blunt: does
this still serve that purpose, within these constraints? If not, it is wrong,
however well it works (\coderef{CONSTITUTION.md}{\gitref}, \S I).

A mirror is not an oracle, and the distinction is load-bearing. Synthesis over the
owner's corpus is \emph{the owner's own material plus model training reflected
back} --- a lens that surfaces patterns, never a source of external truth or
revelation (\coderef{CONSTITUTION.md}{\gitref}, \S III.2). This is the first act of
humility the design makes about itself, and it is why the system is built to hold
its interpretive claims loosely and to say so. An oracle asks to be believed. A
mirror asks only to be accurate.

\begin{principle}[Mirror, not oracle]
The system reflects the owner's mind back to them; it does not stand outside that
mind as an authority. Its worth is measured in fidelity, not in persuasion
(\coderef{CONSTITUTION.md}{\gitref}, \S III.2).
\end{principle}

\section{A system named after its own shape}
\label{sec:ouroboros}

There are two names in play, and keeping them apart is the reader's first piece of
notation. \mindpalace{} is the \emph{framework} --- the repository, the machinery,
the workflow that builds and governs the thing. \Ouroboros{} is the \emph{live
system}: the running daemon together with the evolving corpus it reasons over
(\artifact{dn-ouroboros-principal}, \S 1). The framework is the workshop;
\Ouroboros{} is the machine running.

The name is chosen, not decorative. The ouroboros --- the serpent consuming its
own tail --- is a system that feeds on its own output: it ingests traces of its own
reasoning back into the corpus it reasons over, so that over time it studies a
record that includes itself. That self-reference is the entire ambition of the
project and, simultaneously, its entire danger. A system that reasons about a
corpus containing its own past reasoning can deepen --- or it can slowly launder
its own guesses into apparent ground truth. Nearly every principle that follows
exists to keep the first outcome and forbid the second. How the strata of self-
reference are formally layered is a design now in progress; the mechanism lands in
\autoref{ch:architecture} and its mathematics in \autoref{ch:math}.

\section{One subject, at a distance}
\label{sec:one-subject}

The mirror framing has a sharper reading that the design is still formalising, and
it is worth stating at the level the record already supports. Because the system is
a mirror of \emph{one} mind, everything inside it is, at bottom, about a single
subject --- the owner. A written verdict is the owner directly. Other material is
the owner at a remove: an instrument's reading of them, or the residue of what they
chose to attend to. The corpus was never a neutral sample of the world; it is a
sample of one person's attention (\coderef{CONSTITUTION.md}{\gitref}, \S I;
\coderef{docs/BUILD-SPEC.md}{\gitref}, \S 1).

The design thesis that turns ``at a remove'' into a precise, graded coordinate ---
\fwdthesis{authorship distance}{ch:math} --- is not yet ratified, so this manual
does not lean on its formal claims. What matters philosophically, and is already
settled, is the reframe itself: there is one subject, and the interesting question
about any artifact is not \emph{whose} it is but \emph{how much mediation stands
between the owner and it}. That question is the axis \autoref{ch:math} will draw.

\section{The sacred boundary: the model advises, code acts}
\label{sec:sacred}

If the mirror is the purpose, this is the discipline that protects it. The
governing security lesson, applied everywhere without exception: \emph{the model
advises; code acts}. No language model in the system holds a shell, raw
credentials, or the power to mutate infrastructure directly; capability is granted
only through narrow, audited tools, never through general access
(\coderef{docs/BUILD-SPEC.md}{\gitref}, \S 1 and \S 3, invariant 3;
\coderef{CONSTITUTION.md}{\gitref}, \S II.2). A design that hands a model broad
system reach is a defect on its face.

The word for the boundaries where writes cross into or out of the core is
\emph{sacred}, and the term is easy to misread. It does \emph{not} mean privileged.
It means the opposite: the dangerous, general capability --- a component that could
forge a verdict, an optimiser that could buy past a bright line, a privilege to
mutate the immutable --- is \emph{dissolved} rather than granted under guard. The
design keeps moving the boundary until the dangerous permission is simply
unnecessary. The full taxonomy of the three channels that cross the core's boundary
is architecture; \autoref{ch:architecture} gives it. The principle is here.

\begin{principle}[Dissolve, do not guard]
Sanctity at a boundary is achieved by removing the need for the dangerous
capability, not by permitting it under controls. If a design still requires the
dangerous permission, the boundary is in the wrong place
(\coderef{docs/BUILD-SPEC.md}{\gitref}, \S 3, invariant 3).
\end{principle}

\section{Stop trusting posture; make the property physical}
\label{sec:structural}

A rule that lives only in a prompt or a convention is a rule the system is trusting
itself to keep. The maturing move --- made repeatedly across the design --- is to
stop trusting posture and make the property \emph{physical}, so that a violation is
not \emph{caught} but \emph{unrepresentable}.

The exemplar is the \Ouroboros{} principal (\artifact{dn-ouroboros-principal}).
Several constitution lines that are enforced today by convention --- an agent must
never hold the private corpus, the model never holds raw secrets --- become
\emph{OS-enforced facts} when the live system runs as its own least-privilege
operating-system user: after the change, an agent running as the developer's login
simply \emph{cannot} open the vault. As the note puts it, this is the same move as
the core-seal reckoning: \emph{stop trusting posture, make the property physical}
(\artifact{dn-ouroboros-principal}, \S 2). The mechanism --- the dedicated user, the
launch daemon, the ingest and exhaust lanes --- is architecture, and \autoref{ch:architecture}
carries it; the principle is what belongs here.

The move is not aspirational; parts of it are already concrete in the repository.
The sealed core's isolation from the network is a static lint, not a hope: a
check refuses to let any core module import a networked zone
(\coderef{scripts/check\_imports.py}{\gitref}, the import firewall, invariant 2). And
a minted agent's maximum reachable capability is not described in a paragraph an
agent might rationalise around --- it is a frozen set of one:

\begin{quote}\small\ttfamily
source: core/factory/roles.py@\gitref
\end{quote}
\begin{verbatim}
PRE_DECLARED_MAX: frozenset[str] = frozenset({"run_python"})
\end{verbatim}
\noindent The entire grantable universe for a dynamically minted agent is a single
sandboxed handle; construction refuses any scope that exceeds the maximum
(\coderef{core/factory/roles.py}{\gitref}). ``The model may not touch
infrastructure'' stops being an instruction and becomes a fact about what the code
can express.

\begin{principle}[Structural over behavioural]
A safety property is real only when a mechanism makes its violation impossible or
mechanically caught --- never when it rests on an agent's good behaviour
(\artifact{dn-ouroboros-principal}, \S 2; \coderef{scripts/check\_imports.py}{\gitref}).
\end{principle}

\section{The artifact chain: how a decision becomes real}
\label{sec:artifact-chain}

The same distrust of posture is turned on the \emph{process} that builds the
system, and this is the manual's epistemology. The rule: \emph{no decision,
question, or state lives only in a chat transcript or a session's context window}.
Everything is a typed file with a state machine --- front-matter carrying its type,
its identity, and its status --- so that the state of the project is greppable
rather than remembered (\artifact{dn-agent-workflow}, \S 2.1).

Ideas move one way, through gates (\autoref{fig:artifact-chain}). A brainstorm
becomes a design note; a ratified design note graduates into a build plan; the plan
is executed into a journal and findings; reflection sweeps those back into design.
Crucially, findings are the \emph{only} channel from build back to design, and they
re-enter through the same gate a brainstorm does --- never by side effect
(\artifact{dn-agent-workflow}, \S 3 and \S 11; \coderef{CLAUDE.md}{\gitref}). The
plan itself is not a suggestion but a \emph{capability}: a build plan's write scope
is mechanically enforced, so an agent cannot write outside what its plan authorised
(\artifact{dn-agent-workflow}, \S 2.2). This very manual is written under such a
capability, and it obeys the same rule it is describing.

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \input{figures/artifact-chain}
  \caption[The artifact chain]{The artifact chain: a one-way flow through owner-only
    blessing gates, with findings as the sole channel back into design. No decision
    lives only in a transcript. Source: \artifact{dn-agent-workflow}, \S 3, \S 10.}
  \label{fig:artifact-chain}
\end{figure}

Two gates on that chain are drawn heavier than the rest because they are reserved
for the owner alone, performed by hand, outside any agent session: ratifying a
design note (\textsf{draft}$\to$\textsf{ratified}) and approving a plan split
(\textsf{proposed}$\to$\textsf{ready}). Automating either would slip an
expected-value step inside a bright line, so no command wraps them; they are denied
to every agent pre-hoc and audited post-hoc (\artifact{dn-agent-workflow}, \S 10).
The chain is why this book can be a \emph{derived projection} of the record rather
than an independent account: each claim traces to an artifact id or to code at a
git ref, and a projection with a broken citation is a projection with a bug. It is
also why, when writing this chapter exposed a tension in its own instructions ---
sources named by the plan that the workflow bars from the book --- the response was
to file \artifact{finding-0117} and route it, not to paper over the gap in prose.
Explanation is an audit pass; the audit's findings go where every finding goes.

\section{Ratify falsifiers, not proofs}
\label{sec:falsifiers}

There is a particular epistemic stance threaded through the machinery, and naming
it explains a dozen smaller choices at once. The system does not ask a change to
\emph{prove} itself; it asks what would show the change \emph{wrong}. Every item in
a build plan carries, alongside its acceptance test, a \emph{named falsifier} --- the
observable that would show the item failed or its approach was mistaken --- recorded
as a distinct, first-class field (\artifact{dn-agent-workflow}, \S 3). Acceptance
says ``this passed''; the falsifier says ``here is what passing could not have
looked like.'' Requiring both is the difference between confirming a hope and
testing a claim.

The same stance governs the anchors. The system measures its own behavioural drift
against a \emph{frozen} reference --- the \Constitution{} and the golden baseline
--- rather than scoring itself in the abstract, because a system with no fixed point
can degrade smoothly forever while every individual check still passes
(\coderef{CONSTITUTION.md}{\gitref}, \S IV--\S V;
\coderef{docs/BUILD-SPEC.md}{\gitref}, \S 4). And it governs honesty: never
fabricate a fact, a source, or a citation --- \emph{a cited identifier that does not
resolve is a failure} (\coderef{CONSTITUTION.md}{\gitref}, \S III.1). That single
rule is why this manual re-verifies every snippet and every code reference against
the repository at each edition: an unresolvable citation is not a blemish, it is the
defect the stance is built to catch.

\begin{principle}[Falsifiers over proofs]
A claim earns its place by surviving what would refute it, not by being argued for.
Every plan item names its falsifier; every drift check runs against a frozen anchor;
a citation that does not resolve is a failure (\artifact{dn-agent-workflow}, \S 3;
\coderef{CONSTITUTION.md}{\gitref}, \S III.1, \S IV--\S V).
\end{principle}

\section{Why the fixed point comes first}
\label{sec:fixed-point}

These principles converge on one structural fact, and it is the right note to open
the book on. A self-modifying, self-spawning system with \emph{no} immovable
reference will optimise itself away from its own purpose --- value drift, one
locally acceptable step at a time --- until nothing of the original intent remains
and no single step ever looked wrong (\coderef{CONSTITUTION.md}{\gitref}, \S V;
\coderef{docs/BUILD-SPEC.md}{\gitref}, \S 4). The defence is a fixed point: the
\Constitution{} and the frozen baselines are never auto-modified, changed only by
the owner, deliberately and logged, and every measurement of drift is taken against
them (\autoref{fig:fixed-point}; \coderef{CONSTITUTION.md}{\gitref}, \S V;
\coderef{docs/BUILD-SPEC.md}{\gitref}, \S 3, invariant 9).

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \input{figures/fixed-point}
  \caption[The fixed point]{The immovable centre a self-modifying system measures
    itself against. The self-modification cycle --- propose, human-approve, execute,
    validate, auto-rollback --- may change much, but never the fixed point it is
    judged against. Source: \coderef{CONSTITUTION.md}{\gitref}, \S V;
    \coderef{docs/BUILD-SPEC.md}{\gitref}, \S 4, \S 14.}
  \label{fig:fixed-point}
\end{figure}

This is the resolution of the ouroboros's paradox. The serpent may safely consume
its tail --- the system may ingest its own reasoning, spawn its own agents, and
propose changes to itself --- precisely because there is one thing it may never
digest. Remove the fixed point and you do not get freedom; you get a mirror that
slowly forgets whose face it was built to show. Keep it, and every other capability
in this manual becomes safe to grant.

With the \emph{why} in place, the rest of the book turns to the \emph{how}:
\autoref{ch:architecture} draws the zones and the boundaries that make these
principles physical; \autoref{ch:math} gives the mathematics of grounding and
authorship distance; \autoref{ch:intuition} connects them into a working mental
model; and \autoref{ch:future} records what is deliberately deferred, each parked
decision with the exact condition under which it returns.
